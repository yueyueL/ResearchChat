Welcome to ChatResearch

Your AI-Powered Research Assistant for Scholars and Students

ChatResearch is an AI-powered desktop application designed to assist early-career research students and experienced researchers in their academic pursuits.

Download Now

Why Choose ChatResearch?

As researchers and students, we often grapple with challenging questions. ChatResearch is your AI-powered solution, designed to provide quick, insightful answers to queries like:

  • "What are the recent updates in my research area on arXiv?"
  • "What problems were solved at top conferences like ICML, CVPR, or USENIX Security?"
  • "How can I quickly summarize the key findings of this paper?"
  • "What are the potential research gaps in my field?"
Revolutionize Your Research Process

ChatResearch leverages the power of AI to provide comprehensive insights in minutes, not hours. Our advanced algorithms analyze vast amounts of data to deliver accurate, relevant information tailored to your research needs.

How ChatResearch Works

1. Set Up Your AI API Key

Configure your personal AI usage by securely inputting your API key from providers like OpenAI.

2. Build Your Personal Library

Create a comprehensive collection by uploading papers, using our crawler, or importing from sources like arXiv.

3. Organize Your Research

Manage your papers, add custom tags, create sub-collections, and review paper details for efficient organization.

4. Start Research Chat with AI

Engage in AI-powered discussions about your selected papers, gaining valuable insights and answers.

Download Instructions

You can download ChatResearch from the official GitHub releases page.

On Windows
  • Download the *-win.exe file from the releases page.
  • Run the downloaded file to proceed with the setup.
On macOS
  • For M1, M2, and other Apple Silicon Macs, download the arm64.dmg file.
  • For Intel-based Macs, download the regular .dmg file.
  • When opening the application for the first time, macOS may show a security warning. Follow the instructions in this blog post to allow installation.
On Linux
  • Debian-based Distributions: Download the .deb file from the releases page.
  • Other Distributions: Download the .AppImage file from the releases page.
